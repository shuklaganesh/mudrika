Section 03

Awards Architecture

Ten awards across three conceptual layers — sector awards celebrating domain leadership, stage & character awards honouring the journey, and ecosystem awards recognising the enablers. Together they represent the full breadth of Hyderabad's startup universe.

Award 01 · Sector

Deep Tech

Deep Tech & Innovation Excellence

A startup that has demonstrated genuine technological breakthrough in AI, robotics, space, semiconductors, or advanced engineering — contributing to India's deep tech ambitions from Hyderabad.

Award 02 · Sector

FinTech

FinTech & Financial Inclusion Champion

A startup driving financial access, digital payments, lending innovation, or wealth democratisation — particularly with impact at the grassroots or underserved segments of the population.

Award 03 · Sector

HealthTech

HealthTech & Life Sciences Trailblazer

Recognising breakthrough work in diagnostics, digital health, pharma tech, medical devices, or health access — with tangible patient or community impact at scale.

Award 04 · Sector

Enterprise SaaS

Enterprise SaaS & B2B Excellence

A B2B or SaaS company that has built a scalable product solving real enterprise pain points — demonstrating strong retention, global traction, or genuine category creation.

Award 05 · Sector

Consumer Tech

Consumer Tech & D2C Disruptor

A startup that has reshaped consumer behaviour through technology — in e-commerce, edtech, media, the creator economy, or direct-to-consumer branded ventures with a distinctly Indian identity.

Award 06 · Stage

Early Stage

Best Early Stage Startup (Under 3 Years)

Awarded to a startup under 3 years old that has shown exceptional promise — strong product-market fit signals, early traction, and a clear path to scale. The one to watch.

Award 07 · Stage + Impact

Growth Stage

Growth Stage Startup of the Year

A startup that has successfully navigated from early traction to sustainable growth — demonstrating revenue momentum, team building, and expanding market presence in their chosen domain.

Award 08 · Character

Social Impact

Social Impact & Bharat-First Startup

Recognising a startup whose core mission serves society — rural communities, sustainability, education access, climate, women's empowerment, or underserved populations across Telangana and beyond.

Award 09 · Character

Leadership

Woman Founder of the Year

Celebrating a woman entrepreneur building a formidable venture from Hyderabad — a role model for the next generation of women founders in Telangana and across the country.

Award 10 · Ecosystem

Enabler

Investor / Enabler of the Ecosystem

Awarded to an investor, incubator, accelerator, or institution — T-Hub, WE Hub, TASK, or an Angel Network — that has done the most to strengthen and enable the Hyderabad startup ecosystem this year.

★   Lifetime Achievement Award

The Hyderabad Founder's Legacy Award

The most prestigious recognition of the evening. Awarded to a founder who started their company as a startup and built it into a venture that placed Hyderabad on the global map — creating employment, inspiring a generation of entrepreneurs, and embodying the spirit of building from India for the world. This is not an annual award. It is a recognition of an extraordinary life's work. Presented personally by the Chief Guest, the Hon. Governor of Telangana.

Section 04

Lifetime Achievement — Identification Framework

The following six pillars form the evaluation framework for selecting the Lifetime Achievement awardee. The candidate must score meaningfully on all six. Shortlisting is done by the internal committee.

01

Origin — started as a startup

The venture must have begun as a startup from Hyderabad or with a significant Hyderabad founding story — not an inherited business or one acquired from elsewhere.

02

Global impact or recognition

The company or founder is internationally recognised — listed, globally acquired, operates across multiple countries, or has received international awards or media coverage.

03

Hyderabad pride factor

When people name the venture, they think of Hyderabad. The founder has actively credited the city, invested in local infrastructure, or returned value to the ecosystem.

04

Employment & ecosystem creation

Has created significant direct or indirect employment in the city. Ideally also a mentor, angel investor, or active enabler for future generations of startups.

05

Personal stature & acceptance

The founder must be someone the room — including the Governor — can look up to with genuine admiration. No public controversies. Respected across sectors and generations.

06

Confirmed attendance

A Lifetime Achievement Award loses its power if the recipient is not present. Confirm attendance before finalising the name. This is entirely non-negotiable.

Section 05

Winner Identification & Selection Process

Phase 1

Long-listing

By 2 June 2026

The internal committee identifies 3–5 candidates per award category. Key sources include T-Hub portfolio companies, YourStory Hyderabad, Inc42 regional reports, WE Hub and TASK alumni networks, personal founder networks, and LinkedIn ecosystem research. Yatharth Digital can support with structured digital research for each category.

Phase 2

Evaluation & shortlisting

3–8 June 2026

Score each candidate against five criteria: (1) Revenue / traction evidence, (2) Team quality and depth, (3) Innovation quotient, (4) Hyderabad and Telangana ecosystem contribution, (5) Founder story strength and narrative. The highest scoring candidate per category becomes the confirmed nominee.

Phase 3

Confidential winner confirmation

9–12 June 2026

Quietly reach out to each selected winner to confirm attendance. Brief them on their award category and the event context. Do not disclose to any other party. Collect: high-resolution photograph, 2-line biography, and company logo for citations and stage slides.

Phase 4

Citation writing & material prep

13–18 June 2026

Write one 60-word citation per winner (to be read on stage by the emcee). Prepare trophy inscription text. Prepare award category slides with winner name, company, and category graphic. Yatharth Digital designs all digital materials; Mudrika handles all print and trophy production.

Phase 5

Final rehearsal & pack

22–25 June 2026

Complete emcee script with winner names, citations, and stage directions. Full AV run-through at the venue on June 25. Confirm all 11 trophies and citation certificates are ready. Conduct final attendance confirmation with all winners. Briefing pack distributed to all event-day staff.

Section 09

Critical Protocols & Risk Management

Governor's Office Protocol

Events at Raj Bhavan require careful coordination with the Governor's secretariat. The following must be addressed early and without delay:

Obtain formal event permission letter from Raj Bhavan administration. Coordinate security screening for all 150 guests — the full guest list must be submitted at least 7 days in advance. Confirm the correct protocol for addressing the Governor on stage. Verify photography permissions and any restricted zones within the premises. Confirm VIP parking arrangements and dignitary entry points. Engage Raj Bhavan's dedicated event coordinator as the primary point of contact from day one.

Risk Register & Contingencies

AV failure Always maintain a backup laptop and spare presentation remote. AV vendor conducts a full technical run-through on June 25 at the venue.
Winner no-show Confirm a company representative can collect on behalf of the winner. Emcee script prepared to handle this gracefully without drawing attention.
Chief Guest unavailable Identify a substitute chief guest from the dignitary list in advance. This must be discussed quietly with the dignitary concerned beforehand.
Print delay Mudrika completes all print production at least 7 days before the event. 20 copies of the book held as reserve. Trophies inspected at least 5 days prior.
Time overrun Emcee holds a strict timer. Each speaker is briefed on time limits. A stage manager signals 1-minute warnings backstage. No address to exceed its allotted time.
